What is Facebook?

  • Facebook is an American social networking service owned by the American technology conglomerate Meta Platforms.
  • It was founded in 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg, along with his Harvard College roommates and fellow students Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes.
  • The name Facebook derives from the face book directories often given to American university students.

    • How to Use Facebook?

    Users can create a personal profile and add friends. After adding friends they can post updates, share photos, send messages and join groups. People can also like and comment on posts shared by others.

    How to Create a Facebook Account?

    1. Go to the Facebook Website
    2. Click on Sign Up
    3. Enter your name, email or phone number and password.
    4. Select your date of birth and gender.
    5. Click Create Account.
    6. Verify your email or phone number.

    History

    Facebook started as a college project by Mark Zuckerberg. Before it, he created a site called Facemash at Harvard University. In 2004, he launched “TheFacebook” with help from friends, first only for Harvard students, then expanded to other universities and later everyone above 13. The company grew fast, added features like News Feed, attracted investments, and became the most popular social platform, even surpassing MySpace. It earned money through ads and went public in 2012. Over time, Facebook bought platforms like Instagram and WhatsApp. However, it faced issues like the Cambridge Analytica scandal. In 2021, it rebranded to Meta to focus on virtual reality and the metaverse.

    Journey

    • 2004 - Facebook launched at Harvard.
    • 2005 - Expanded to other universities.
    • 2006 - Opened for everyone above 13 years.
    • 2012 - Reached 1 billion users.
    • 2021 - Company name changed to Meta.
